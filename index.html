Project Scope

Literature Survey

Skin disease is one of the most common and difficult disease for diagnosis because of its lack of awareness and ignorance. The importance of recognizing and treating skin disease at an early stage is important, as the skin protects the human body from fungal and bacterial infections. Many people develop skin diseases because of their job, lack of nutrition, regular habitats, and exposure to chemicals, among other factors. Climate, summer season, and winter season are all factors that influence the presence of skin disease. As a result, detecting and diagnosing skin disease at an early stage is crucial.Many skin diseases turn into serious problems if they are not properly treated. As a result, it's critical to regularly monitor and detect skin diseases so that correct treatment and recovery procedures may be performed. For take the proper medical attention we must first identify the disease and the best doctor options they have. Therefore, we have proposed this system which will predict and classify the disease, identify the impact level of the skin diseases by getting the progression, recommend best doctors by mainly considering patient reviews, community based dermatological prevention guidelines and predict the future risk through the symptomatic prognosis.

Research Gap

There are several research papers which are proposed on machine learning and image processing-based skin disease detection and classification detection systems. Alam, Md & Munia, Tamanna Tabassum Khan & Tavakolian, Kouhyar & Vasefi, Fartash & MacKinnon, Nicholas & Fazel-Rezai, Reza, “Automatic Detection and Severity Measurement of Eczema Using Image Processing”, proposed a computer program and image processing technique for automatic eczema detection and severity measurement. In essence, the method allowed users to identify and assess the severity of eczema by taking a picture of the affected skin area. To distinguish between mild and severe eczema, our system used picture segmentation, feature extraction, and statistical classification. An index of severity was given to the photograph when the type of eczema was determined. The implementation of several popular segmentation techniques, including Otsu's, watershed, and region-growing segmentation, led to the usage of colour-based segmentation using k-means clustering because none of these techniques produced the intended results. Colour, texture, and border features served as the foundation for feature extraction, and the SVM classifier approach was used for classification. Sumithra R, Mahamad Suhil, D. S. Guru, “Segmentation and Classification of Skin Lesions for Disease Diagnosis”, proposed an approach for automatically segmenting and categorizing skin lesions. Initially, visible skin hair and noise were removed from skin image pre-processing. Following the filtering process, these images were divided using the regiongrowing segmentation method, and colour and texture feature extraction was carried out. Support Vector Machine (SVM) and K-Nearest Neighbor (KNN) classifier algorithms were used to identify a specific disease from the photos based on the collected features. Better performance outcomes are achieved by this system's combination of SVM and KNN techniques rather than either one alone

Methodology

Figure 2. High Level Architecture of the system.

The proposed system consists of 4 main components. They are;

  1. Face Skin Disease Prediction and Classification with Anomaly Detection
  2. Disease Visualization with Impact Level and Progression
  3. Doctor Recommendation and Community Based Dermatological Prevention
  4. Predict the Future Risk through the Symptomatic Prognosis

The proposed solution intends to provide a smart approach for patients, to detect the skin diseases that may affect the human face skin. As shown in Fig. 1, the registered users of the system can capture or upload the images which are infected. The main purpose of this component is to detect and classify a skin disease when the user uploaded a specific image of their face to the system along with anomaly detection. To reach this goal deep learning approach called Xception model have been used. As this proposed system identify the skin diseases this will also give patients the ability to identify the impact level of the disease, visualize the identified disease, check progression with the previous medical data of the patient, get best doctor recommendation options by patient review analysis and let users access a patient community where they can post their dermatological questions and concerns to get answers. And also this system will provide the ability to predict the future risk through symptomatic prognosis and image analysis for an exact time period.
